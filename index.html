The Problem

The bottleneck isn't pipeline — it's rep capacity. 100% inbound means every new call interrupts the next one. Volume is arriving faster than reps can work through it, and the consequence isn't lost pipeline — it's slowed velocity and compounding rep fatigue.

Hiring scales linearly and expensively. Adding headcount buys time, not leverage. The real lever is deflecting the predictable portion of the deal cycle — the part that follows a script — so reps stay on the work that actually requires judgment.

The strategic question: optimize for net-new logos or ACV expansion? My read is net-new logos. The inbound volume is the asset. Right now it's being starved by rep time-on-task. The opportunity is to make that volume convertible without proportional rep involvement.

The Tiered Motion
T1
Ready to self-serve

Route directly to Teams signup. No rep involved. Friction here is a cost, not a feature.

T2
Enterprise-ready, no questions

Immediate automated response: a direct link to Enterprise checkout. No rep, no call scheduled. The buyer completes it on their own — a rep only steps in if the checkout breaks.

T3
Highest leverage
Has questions

Routed to the chatbot — no rep scheduled, no call to book. The chatbot handles the questions that currently fill rep calendars: pricing at every tier (seat counts, token commitments, spend thresholds, volume discounts), security posture, architecture, model training, use cases. Public documentation served inline. NDA click-through unlocks deeper security docs and architecture detail. ToS available directly. Deep or custom security questions submitted through the chat and answered async by the SMB team. Rep calendar stays clear.

T4
Human rep

Reserved for complex enterprise, strategic logos, multi-stakeholder deals, procurement navigation, and expansions. The rep is a closer and relationship owner — not a Q&A machine.

The common thread: AI handles assembly, humans handle judgment. Every workflow above replaces a 30-to-90-minute manual task with a 30-second prompt. Across a week, that's hours back — hours I spend on calls, in accounts, closing deals. That's the same frame I'd bring to the inbound motion at Anthropic.

  • What's current time-from-inbound-to-closed-won, and which stage is the longest?
  • What has the team already tried on the automation side, and where did it fall short?
